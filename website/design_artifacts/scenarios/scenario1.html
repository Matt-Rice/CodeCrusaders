Scenario 1

Medication Management

James, a retired accountant that lives alone and has two sons, has recently had a medical scare where his heart entered a state of atrial fibrillation, and his doctor wrote him several prescriptions for heart medicines that need to be taken several times a day at very specific times.

In the past, James had taken pain medication for a back surgery he had a few years ago, but he did not have to worry about what time he took those pills as he just had to take them as needed and it was fine if he missed taking medicine at a certain time. Now, his doctor prescribed him with two medicines for his high blood pressure and high cholesterol in an effort to lessen the chance of a heart attack that need to be taken once at 7AM and once at 7PM. His doctor warned him that missing several doses will lead to an increased chance of a heart attack. He wants to be able to set reminders on his phone to take his medicine that is persistent and will send his two sons when he takes his medicine.

James has tried the standard alarm and reminder settings and has found that it is not very persistent, and it does not have a setting that allows him to enter detailed information on his medicine (how many pills to take and what the pills look like) and does not have a button to confirm that he has taken his medicine and does not notify others that the medicine has been taken.

James wants to be able to use an app on his phone that will allow him to enter detailed information about the medicine he needs to take, set reminders for what time he needs to take the pills at. These reminders will send out constant loud notifications until medicine is taken so that he will make sure to take them. After taking the medicine, he will be able to click a confirm button that will send a message to anybody he chooses (his sons and maybe his doctor) alerting them that he took his medicine.
